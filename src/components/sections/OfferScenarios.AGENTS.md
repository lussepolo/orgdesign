# Offer Scenarios Semantic Guide

## Platform Purpose

The Offer Scenarios tab is a board-facing decision console for Rio campus launch scenarios.
It is not only a staffing table.

The platform helps leadership compare what each scenario activates, requires, postpones, and pressures. It should clarify the meaning of each scenario before the conversation turns into a list of roles or isolated payroll lines.

## Core Board Questions

The platform should help answer four questions for each scenario:

- What was originally budgeted?
- What are we now recommending?
- What is the incremental budget impact?
- Why is the increment necessary?

## Scenario Ladder Logic

### Scenario A

- Up to Grade 3.
- Basic offer path.
- Provides the base of the school experience.
- Includes classroom package, MAP from Grade 1, PDJ in classroom routine, and Learning Experience Design.
- Does not provide robust academic differentiation support.
- Should not be framed as a strong quality-protection scenario.

### Scenario B

- Up to Grade 4.
- This is where the offer begins to improve through the Lower School Researchers learning engine.
- Project Design Journey remains full-class, but becomes more academically rigorous through inquiry, evidence-making, Math reasoning, and Scientific Literacy.
- Strengthens documentation, academic evidence, research routines, academic language, and Concept identity.
- Learner habits may be referenced only as part of research routines, not as the main driver of the scenario.
- No Passion Projects yet.

### Scenario C

- Up to Grade 5.
- Completes Lower School.
- Activates Pathways and transition protocols.
- Projects remain full-class experiential projects.
- Passion Projects are not active yet.
- Project Mentorship begins only with the Grade 6 Passion Project model.

### Scenario D

- Up to Grade 6.
- Marks a shift toward Middle School operating rhythm.
- Activates Passion Projects, small-group learner-led work, clusters, advisory, Creative Hub, MUN, academic electives, and project mentorship.
- Should not be visually spotlighted as the preferred option.
- Should be treated as one scenario among others, with equal visual weight.

## Scenario Transition Semantics

Scenario changes are not only grade-ceiling changes. Each transition changes the offer, the adult system, the program layer, the academic rigor expectation, and the budget pressure.

### A -> B: From basic offer to stronger academic progression

Scenario A is the basic offer path. It establishes the base of the school experience: classroom package, MAP from Grade 1, PDJ in classroom routine, and Learning Experience Design. It does not yet provide robust academic differentiation support.

Scenario B is where the offer begins to improve. Grade 4 deepens the Lower School Researchers learning engine: learners are expected to document more carefully, reason with evidence, strengthen Math and Scientific Literacy, use academic language more intentionally, and engage in more rigorous full-class inquiry through PDJ. Passion Projects are not active yet.

### B -> C: From academic progression to Lower School completion

Scenario C completes Lower School. Grade 5 activates Pathways and transition protocols. Projects remain full-class experiential projects. Passion Projects are still not active. Project Mentorship should not be treated as active before Grade 6.

### C -> D: From Lower School completion to Middle School operating rhythm

Scenario D activates Grade 6 and shifts the model toward Middle School rhythm. Passion Projects begin, usually in small groups of 3 to 5 learners. Cluster logic, advisory, Creative Hub, MUN, academic electives, and project mentorship become relevant. This is an operating-model shift, but Scenario D should not be visually or semantically privileged as the preferred scenario.

## Classroom Package Semantics

Classroom package has a precise meaning:

- EY classroom package = reference educator + assistant + monitor.
- LS classroom package = reference educator + assistant.

Classroom package is not the broader support ecosystem.
Do not use vague terms such as "educator and classroom support" when the intended meaning is classroom package.

## Broader Support Ecosystem

Broader support roles are separate from classroom package. This ecosystem includes:

- leadership
- Learning Experience Design
- counseling
- academic/language support
- specialists
- After School coordination
- project mentorship or program coordination

Do not merge classroom package language with broader support ecosystem language.

## Learning Engine Semantics

The Offer Scenarios platform must not treat scenarios as grade-ceiling changes only. Each scenario also represents how Escola Concept's divisional learning engines become visible through the offer.

Learning-engine taxonomy:

- Early Years = Explorers
- Lower School = Researchers
- Middle School = Designers
- High School = Innovative Global Citizens

Project Design Journey is translated differently depending on the divisional learning engine.

### Explorers, Early Years

PDJ appears through curiosity, sensory investigation, play, care routines, environment, language emergence, and documentation of discovery.

### Researchers, Lower School

PDJ becomes more academically explicit through inquiry, observation, evidence-making, Math reasoning, Scientific Literacy, documentation, academic language, and research routines.

### Designers, Middle School

PDJ shifts toward learner agency, iteration, small-group project work, Passion Projects, mentorship, prototyping, critique, and more complex problem framing.

### Innovative Global Citizens, High School

PDJ connects to future-facing citizenship, disciplinary depth, external impact, entrepreneurship, research, global competencies, and public contribution.

### Scenario A and Learning Engines

Scenario A includes Early Years and Lower School up to Grade 3.

It establishes the basic offer across:

- Explorers in Early Years
- early Researchers in Lower School

It should not be framed as robust quality protection or robust academic differentiation. It provides the base experience, but the Researchers engine is still in its early stage.

### Scenario B and Learning Engines

Scenario B adds Grade 4.

This is where the offer begins to improve because the Lower School Researchers engine becomes more academically visible.

Do not frame Scenario B mainly around "learner habits." Learner habits can be mentioned as secondary, but the main semantic shift is academic and epistemic:

- stronger inquiry
- evidence-making
- Math reasoning
- Scientific Literacy
- documentation quality
- academic language
- research routines
- Concept identity as a learner-researcher

PDJ remains full-class. Passion Projects are not active yet.

### Scenario C and Learning Engines

Scenario C adds Grade 5 and completes Lower School.

The Researchers engine reaches a stronger pre-Middle School maturity.

Grade 5 activates:

- Pathways
- transition protocols
- stronger portfolio evidence
- readiness routines
- academic evidence for transition into Grade 6

Projects remain full-class experiential projects.
Passion Projects and Project Mentorship are not active yet.

### Scenario D and Learning Engines

Scenario D adds Grade 6.

This is where the platform begins the transition from the Lower School Researchers engine to the Middle School Designers engine.

Grade 6 activates:

- Passion Projects
- small-group learner-led projects, typically 3-5 learners
- project mentorship
- clusters
- advisory
- Creative Hub
- MUN
- academic electives
- stronger iteration, critique, and learner agency

Scenario D should still not be visually or semantically privileged as the preferred scenario. It is an operating-model shift, not an automatic recommendation.

## Role Maturation Semantics

Recommended functions can mature across scenarios. A function may begin as baseline, shared, recommended, or hybrid before becoming a dedicated role.

Role maturity states:

- baseline role: already present in the original budget/salary basis
- shared or hybrid function: responsibility absorbed by an existing role or shared across roles
- recommended function: strategically needed, but not necessarily a dedicated FTE yet
- dedicated role: explicit role or FTE required for the scenario to operate with fidelity
- increment: anything exceeding the original budget basis through new role, added FTE, higher salary band, broader scope, expanded coverage, or reclassification

| Function | Scenario A | Scenario B | Scenario C | Scenario D |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Learning Experience Design | Baseline | Baseline, more important as documentation deepens | Baseline, supports Pathways and transition evidence | Baseline, interfaces with Middle School rhythm but is not Project Mentorship |
| LAP / Language Acquisition Coach | To validate / recommended if academic-language risk is high | More relevant as Math reasoning, Scientific Literacy, bilingual evidence, academic language, and research routines become more visible | Strongly recommended as Grade 5 readiness pressure grows | May require dedicated support depending on academic rigor and bilingual monitoring expectations |
| Project Mentorship | Not active | Not active | Not active | Begins with Grade 6 Passion Projects, may be coordinated before becoming dedicated FTE |
| Specialists | 1 + 1 + 1 baseline | Load pressure begins as offer expands | Program pressure grows with Pathways and Grade 5 routines | Expanded capacity likely if Creative Hub, MUN, electives, and Middle School rhythm activate |
| After School Coordinator | Existing repo basis is After School Educator, coordinator scope not confirmed | Scope pressure grows with family routines and enrollment | Scope pressure grows with expanded offering | Validate whether coordinator scope is new role, upgrade, or reclassification |

## Classroom Capacity Semantics

Scenario decisions are not only about grade ceiling. They also affect:

- number of sections
- classroom package load
- specialist schedule load
- adult-to-learner routines
- supervision and transitions
- feasibility of program offerings
- whether an existing role can remain hybrid/shared or must become dedicated

Classroom capacity rules:

- EY classroom package = reference educator + assistant + monitor.
- LS classroom package = reference educator + assistant.
- Classroom package is baseline and should not be treated as an increment unless quantity, FTE, salary band, or coverage changes.
- Classroom package is not the broader support ecosystem.

### Scenario A

- Basic offer path.
- No Pathways, Creative Hub, MUN, Passion Projects, Middle School advisory, or academic electives.
- Focuses on base school experience, classroom routine, MAP, PDJ, documentation, and Learning Experience Design.

### Scenario B

- Grade 4 strengthens the Lower School Researchers engine.
- PDJ remains full-class, but becomes more academically rigorous through inquiry, evidence-making, Math reasoning, Scientific Literacy, and academic language.
- Documentation and academic evidence become more intentional.
- Still no Passion Projects.

### Scenario C

- Grade 5 activates Pathways and transition protocols.
- Projects remain full-class.
- Passion Projects are not active yet.
- Project Mentorship is not active yet.

### Scenario D

- Grade 6 activates Middle School rhythm.
- Passion Projects, small-group learner-led work, clusters, advisory, Creative Hub, MUN, academic electives, and project mentorship become relevant.

## Academic Rigor Semantics

Academic rigor grows across scenarios through:

- MAP from Grade 1
- bilingual monitoring
- documentation quality
- academic evidence
- learner habits
- intervention and enrichment
- Pathways
- transition protocols
- family-facing academic evidence
- academic/language support

Scenario A establishes the basic offer and early academic evidence, but does not yet provide robust support for academic differentiation.

Scenario B is where the offer begins to improve through the Lower School Researchers engine: stronger inquiry routines, Math reasoning, Scientific Literacy, academic language, documentation quality, and evidence-based learning.

Scenario C strengthens pre-Middle School readiness through Pathways and transition routines.

Scenario D changes the operating model and requires more coordination across academic rigor, program offerings, and learner-led project work.

## Baseline vs Increment Logic

Baseline = already present in the original salary/budget basis.

Increment = anything that exceeds the original basis through a new role, added FTE, higher salary band, broader scope, expanded coverage, or reclassification.

Known baseline assumptions:

- EY classroom package.
- LS classroom package.
- Division Principal / division leadership basis, subject to title mapping.
- Learning Experience Designer.
- Specialist baseline: 1 Body & Movement + 1 Arts + 1 Music.

Known ambiguity:

- Repo includes After School Educator, HC 1 from 2028.
- After School Coordinator scope is not confirmed as originally budgeted.
- No increment only if Finance/HR confirms the existing After School Educator covers the full coordinator scope.
- Otherwise, After School Coordinator is an increment, scope upgrade, or reclassification.

Likely increment areas:

- Specialist capacity beyond 1 Body & Movement + 1 Arts + 1 Music.
- Language Acquisition Coach / LAP Coach / academic-language support unless already budgeted.
- Creative Hub / Design Technologies / Makerspace capacity unless covered by existing roles.
- Project mentorship or program coordination if Scenario D requires dedicated capacity.
- Scope expansion of already budgeted roles.

## Learning Experience Design Semantics

Learning Experience Designer is baseline, not a new increment.

Its role is to support PDJ fidelity, documentation quality, learning-engine translation, and consistency of experiential learning.

Learning Experience Design should not be confused with Project Mentorship.
Project Mentorship becomes relevant with Grade 6 Passion Projects.

## Specialist Capacity Logic

- Original specialist baseline is only 1 Body & Movement, 1 Arts, 1 Music.
- Any specialist expansion beyond 1 + 1 + 1 is a potential increment.
- Specialist capacity should be interpreted through load, schedule, space, and program ambition.
- Do not assume all specialist expansion is already covered.

## Transition Behavior Rule

Scenario transitions should feel smooth in the interface, but semantically distinct in the model. Do not blur A, B, C, and D into a generic expansion ladder.

## UX Behavior Rules

- The hero should frame comparison, not advocate for Scenario D.
- Scenario D should not be persistently spotlighted.
- All four scenario cards should have equal visual weight.
- The executive synthesis should begin with Scenario A.
- Budget comparison should separate original baseline from current recommendation and increment.
- Avoid mixing English and Portuguese unless the English term is an official program name, such as Pathways, Creative Hub, Passion Projects, or MUN.

## Do-Not-Change Rules

- Do not change scenario logic without updating this file.
- Do not treat classroom package as an increment.
- Do not treat Learning Experience Designer as an increment.
- Do not treat Division Principal as an increment unless title mapping changes.
- Do not treat After School Coordinator as baseline unless Finance/HR confirms it maps to After School Educator.
- Do not frame Scenario A as robust quality protection.
- Do not visually privilege Scenario D.
- Do not merge classroom package and support ecosystem language.
- Do not start Passion Projects before Grade 6.
- Do not activate Project Mentorship before Grade 6.
- Do not describe Grade 5 Pathways as Passion Projects.
- Do not treat a shared/hybrid function as a dedicated FTE unless Finance/HR or leadership validates it.
- Do not change role maturity state, baseline, shared, recommended, dedicated, without updating this file.
- Do not change classroom capacity assumptions without checking specialist load and program offering implications.
